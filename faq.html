Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about procedures, recovery, and care

What is the recovery time for knee replacement surgery?

Most patients can resume normal activities within 6-12 weeks after knee replacement. However, complete recovery and maximum benefit can take up to 6 months. Physical therapy plays a crucial role in achieving optimal results.

Timeline:

  • Week 1-2: Rest and basic physical therapy
  • Week 3-4: Walking with crutches, therapy intensifies
  • Week 6-8: Weight-bearing exercises, light activities
  • Week 12+: Return to moderate activities

Is knee replacement surgery painful?

Modern surgical techniques and anesthesia make the surgery itself painless. Post-surgery, patients experience some discomfort which is well-managed with pain medications. Most patients report that the pain relief from the surgery far outweighs post-operative discomfort.

Our team focuses on pain management through:

  • Advanced anesthesia techniques
  • Regional nerve blocks for pain management
  • Effective pain medications
  • Ice and elevation strategies

What are the risks associated with orthopedic surgery?

Like all surgical procedures, orthopedic surgery carries some risks. However, serious complications are rare, especially when performed by experienced surgeons at well-equipped hospitals.

Common minor complications:

  • Swelling and bruising (temporary)
  • Mild pain (managed with medication)
  • Temporary stiffness

Rare serious complications: Blood clots, infection, or nerve injury. Our hospital follows strict protocols to prevent these.

When can I start physical therapy after surgery?

Physical therapy typically begins the same day or day after surgery. Early mobilization is key to successful recovery.

Initial therapy includes:

  • Gentle range of motion exercises in bed
  • Walking with support (crutches or walker)
  • Ankle pumps to prevent blood clots
  • Breathing exercises

Our physiotherapy team will create a personalized rehabilitation plan for optimal recovery.

What activities should I avoid after surgery?

Activities to avoid (first 6-12 weeks):

  • High-impact exercises (running, jumping)
  • Heavy lifting (more than 10 lbs)
  • Bending excessively
  • Driving while on pain medication
  • Contact sports

Safe activities: Walking, swimming (after clearance), yoga, gentle cycling, and daily activities.

Always follow your doctor's specific recommendations as recovery varies by individual.

How long do I need to take medications after surgery?

This depends on the type of surgery and your recovery progress. Generally:

  • Pain medications: Usually tapered over 2-6 weeks
  • Antibiotics: Typically 2-7 days post-surgery
  • Blood thinners: May be prescribed for 10-14 days to prevent clots
  • Anti-inflammatory: Often used for 2-4 weeks

Never stop medications without consulting your doctor. Gradual reduction is important for safe recovery.

Is orthopedic surgery covered by health insurance?

Most comprehensive health insurance policies cover orthopedic surgeries. However, coverage varies based on:

  • Your specific insurance plan
  • Whether the surgery is deemed medically necessary
  • Hospital network status
  • Pre-authorization requirements

We accept most major insurance providers. Our billing team can verify your coverage and help with pre-authorization paperwork.

Can I continue my current medications before surgery?

Some medications should be continued while others must be stopped before surgery. Common medications to discuss:

  • Blood thinners: Usually stop 3-7 days before surgery
  • Diabetes medications: Adjusted based on fasting requirements
  • Blood pressure meds: Often continued with small sip of water
  • Over-the-counter pain relievers: Generally stop 1 week before

Important: Always inform your doctor about all medications, supplements, and herbal products you take.
